    Talking about the temples, Kathmandu the capital city of Nepal is known as the city of temples. These temples represent the country's diverse religious landscape, with influences from Hinduism, Buddhism, and other spiritual practices. From the majestic stupas in the Kathmandu Valley to the sacred shrines nestled in the Himalayas, Nepal's temples offer a fascinating glimpse into the nation's past and present spiritual practices.
    Some of the famous temples of Nepal are:
    • Pashupatinath Mandir - The heart of Hinduism in Nepal

      • Located on the banks of the Bagmati River in Kathmandu, the Pashupatinath Temple is one of the holiest Hindu temples in Nepal and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Dedicated to Lord Shiva, this temple attracts thousands of pilgrims, especially during the Maha Shivaratri festival. The temple’s architecture is a beautiful blend of pagoda-style and traditional Nepali design. The temple complex also includes several smaller shrines, ghats, and cremation areas, making it a significant spiritual and cultural hub in Nepal.
      Significance
      : The temple is considered one of the holiest pilgrimage sites for Hindus, and the vibrant atmosphere during religious ceremonies is truly unforgettable.
  • Swayambhunath Stupa (Monkey Temple) – A Symbol of Tibetan Buddhism
  • Boudhanath Stupa – The Largest Tibetan Stupa
  • Lumbini – The Birthplace of Lord Buddha
  • Changu Narayan Temple – The Oldest Hindu Temple
  • Balkumari Temple – A Hidden Gem in the Kathmandu Valley
  • Muktinath Temple – A Sacred Shrine in the Himalayas
